Mechanics of system of particles

Term Description
Shm Differential equation of SHM is d2x dt2 + ω2x = 0
Solutions of this equation can both be sine or cosine functions.We conveniently choose x = Acos(ωt + φ) where A,φ and ω all are constants
Amplitude Quantity A is known of SHM which is the magnitude of maximum value of displacement on either sides from the equilibrum position
Time period Time period (T) of SHM the time during whichoscillation repeat itself i.e., repeatsits one cycle of motion and it is given by 2n ω where ω is the angular frequency
Frequency Frequency of the SHM is the number of the complete oscillation per unit time i.e., frequency is reciprocal of the time period f=1/T.thus angular frequency ω=2πf
Velocity Velocity of a system executing SHM as a function of time is v = - ωAsin(ωt+φ)
Accelceration Acceleration of particle executing SHM is a = -ω2Acos(ωt+φ) so a = -ω2x
THis shows that acceleration is proportional to the displacement but in opposite direction
Kinetic Energy At any time t KE of system in SHM is KE = (1/2)mv2 = (1/2)mω2A2sin2(ωt+φ) which is a function varying periodically in time
Potential Energy PE of system in SHM at any time t is PE = (1/2)kx2 = (1/2)mω2A2cos2(ωt+φ)
Total Energy Total energy in SHm E = KE+PE = (1/2)mω2A2
and it remain constant in absence of dissipative forces like frictional forces
Oscillations of a Spring mass system In this case particle of mass m oscillates under the influence of hook's law restoring force given by F = -kx where k is the spring constant
Angular Frequency ω = √(k/m)
Time period T= 2n√(m/k) frequency is = (1/2n)√(k/m) Time period of both horizontal and vertical osciallation are same but spring constant have different value for horizontal and vertical motion

Compound Pendulum Compund pendulum is a rigid body of any shpae capable of oscillating about the horizontal axis passing through it.
such a pendulum swinging with small angle executes SHM with the time period
T = 2n√( I mqL ) where I = moment of inertia of pendulum about the axis of suspension L is the length of the pendulum
Damped Oscillation SHM which continues indefinately without the loss of the amplitude are called free oscillation or undamped and it is not a real case.
In real physical systems energy of the oscillator gradully decreases with time and oscillator will eventually come to rest.this happens becuase in actual physical systems, friction (or damping) is always present
The reduction in amplitude or energy of the oscillator is called damping and oscillation are called damped
Forced Oscillation and Response Oscillations of a system under the influence of an external periodic force are called forced oscillations
If frequency of externally applied driving force is equal to the natural frequency of the oscillator resonance is said to occur
